SAP Developments — Week of Feb 23–28
AI Infrastructure Bifurcation: CoreWeave −20% vs Dell +21.8% — What It Means for SAP's Narrative
CoreWeave's first post-IPO report disappointed on Q1 guidance — raising questions about whether hyperscaler AI capex momentum is sustainable. This directly intersects with SAP's Joule AI narrative: if customers see hyperscalers pulling back on AI investment, the ROI urgency for enterprise AI tools softens. Conversely, Dell's blowout on AI server demand signals infrastructure layer is still accelerating. SAP is positioned in the application layer above infrastructure — meaning it benefits from infrastructure buildout (more compute for Joule) but is at risk if the business case narrative for enterprise AI weakens. Monitor Joule attach rates in Q1 2026 results for signal.
Argentina Labor Reform Signed — Direct Relevance to SAP LATAM Deal Cycles
Senate passed Ley de Modernización Laboral (42–28) early Saturday. Key provisions: reduced employer contributions (1pt large / 2.5pt SME), flexible hours, reduced dismissal cost base, gig platform statute. This is structurally positive for Argentine enterprise customers' ERP investment capacity. Lower labor cost burden = more capital available for tech stack modernization. SAP mid-market expansion in Argentina's formal economy could see deal cycle improvement in H2 2026 as labor reform bedded in. Factor into Argentina pipeline probability assumptions for Q2–Q3.
Q4/FY2025 CCB Miss — Still the Core Risk Context for ERM in Q1 2026
Current Cloud Backlog grew only 16% vs analyst consensus of 26% — shares fell 14% on Jan 29 earnings day, largest single-day drop since Oct 2020. Root cause: sovereign cloud deals with government termination-for-convenience clauses excluded from backlog by accounting definition. CFO Asam: "In some countries, in some industries, customers have more questions around sovereignty." €10B buyback active Feb 2026. FY2026 guidance: Cloud €25.8–26.2B. The CCB miss is a backlog recognition lag, not a demand collapse — but it creates a Q1 2026 narrative risk if deals continue to take longer to close.
Block's 50% Workforce Cut as AI Template — ERM Scenario for SAP's Customer Base
Block (Jack Dorsey) announced layoffs of nearly 4,200 employees — roughly half its workforce — framing it explicitly as AI-driven productivity replacement. Stock +16.8% on the day. This is the clearest datapoint yet that markets are rewarding AI workforce substitution at scale. For SAP ERM: risk that large SAP customers begin similar restructuring, potentially altering HR module volumes, talent management demand, and payroll complexity (fewer headcount = simpler HRIS needs). Counterpoint: AI restructuring often requires sophisticated HRIS to manage transitions. Model both directions for HR module ARR sensitivity analysis.
EU AI Act — 155 Days to HRAI Deadline. Joule Classification Assessment Still Required.
Commission missed Article 6 guidance deadline Feb 2. Do not wait. Joule embedded in finance (FP&A, cash flow prediction), HR (talent scoring, workforce planning), and creditworthiness contexts maps to Annex III high-risk categories.
